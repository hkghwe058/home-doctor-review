## What Is The Home Doctor Book?

The Home Doctor is a 304-page paperback that teaches ordinary people how to handle medical situations when professional help isn't available. Not "when you have a cold and don't feel like driving to the clinic." More like: when the pharmacy shelves are empty, the power's been out for a week, and there's no ambulance coming.

That sounds dramatic. But the book was born out of exactly that reality.

Dr. Maybell Nieves worked as a surgeon in Caracas, Venezuela, before and during the country's economic collapse. When hospitals ran out of basic supplies — antibiotics, anesthesia, even electricity — she and her colleagues had to invent new ways to treat patients. Ways that didn't depend on a functioning medical system.

Those methods are what fill the pages of this book. They're specifically designed to be applied by non-medical people, using supplies that most households can reasonably stockpile.

## What's Actually Inside the 304 Pages?

I'm not going to list every chapter. But here's what the coverage looks like, organized by the kind of situation you might face:

**Emergency Recognition**
How to identify a heart attack (four distinctive symptoms to watch for), how to quickly recognize a stroke and what to do immediately, and how to tell if an irregular heartbeat is benign or dangerous. These sections alone could change an outcome.

**Medication Management**
A deep dive into which expired medications are still safe — and which aren't. Venezuela, according to the book, became the world's largest unintentional experiment in expired medicine. The findings are genuinely surprising. There's also a guide to legally stockpiling four specific antibiotics, plus an ingenious method for building a reserve of prescription medicines like insulin.

**Wound Care and Skin Conditions**
From cleaning and stitching an open wound at home to managing burns, abscesses, fungal infections, insect bites, and removing ingrown nails. Chapter 3 covers nearly every skin and wound scenario a household might encounter.

**Pain and Chronic Issues**
A one-minute stretch routine for back and neck pain that Dr. Maybell developed for herself. Abdominal pain diagnosis using a nine-section palpation diagram. Migraine identification and management. And how to recognize hidden internal inflammation through little-known body signs.

**Natural and Household Remedies**
Cabbage wraps for inflammation (taught in medical school, not folk medicine). Listerine as a surgical antiseptic — it was originally formulated for exactly that purpose in 1879. The antibiotic plant Usnea (Old Man's Beard) that grows across North America, and how to turn it into a tincture.

**Infection and Illness Management**
An at-home protocol for flu and respiratory issues when going to a hospital isn't an option. Dental infection care when you can't see a dentist. Ear infection relief using a common sugar-free gum ingredient.

That's maybe half of what's in there.
